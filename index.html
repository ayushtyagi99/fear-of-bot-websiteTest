About the team

Frederick, Maryland

Fear of Bot is a rookie FIRST Tech Challenge team made up of students from 8th through 11th grade. We're a group of friends driven by a genuine passion for robotics — committed to achieving competitive results while enjoying every step of the process.

This season challenged us to adapt, iterate, and grow — both as engineers and as people. We designed and built our robot from the ground up, developed an autonomous program from scratch, and competed in the Decode 2025–26 game challenge.

The machine

Every subsystem on our robot — from the mecanum drivetrain to the vision-guided shooter — was designed, built, and programmed by our student members. We iterated through three major versions of the intake, shooter, and transfer systems across the season.

Android Studio + Java
Core IDE and language for all robot programming
Software
Pedro Pathing
Bézier curve trajectory planning for autonomous
Auto
Subsystems → Commands → OpModes
Modular architecture: subsystems encapsulate hardware, commands handle logic, OpModes run the match
Architecture
FTC Dashboard + PIDF Tuning
Real-time tuning of PID values and servo positions without re-deploying code
Tuning
Pinpoint Odometry (2 Dead Wheels)
X/Y position and heading tracking for precise autonomous movement
Localization
👁
Vision
Limelight 3A Camera
Identifies motif patterns, calculates distance from goal, and assists with accurate shot alignment via AprilTags.
⚙️
Intake
GoBilda Gecko Wheels
4 equally-spaced Gecko Wheels driven by a 1150 RPM motor. "Touch and Own" design for consistent intake regardless of approach angle.
🔁
Transfer
Rubber Band Drive + Torque Servo
3D-printed brackets and rubber bands advance artifacts along the ramp. Torque servo holds and launches. Three REV 2M distance sensors track ball count.
🎯
Shooter
GoBilda Rhino Wheel
Single flywheel design chosen over dual for less weight, higher accuracy, and reduced power consumption. PETG side plates for structural strength.
🚗
Drivetrain
Parallel Plate + Odometry Pod
Custom parallel-plate chassis with mecanum wheels and GoBilda odometry pods for precise position tracking. X-configuration eliminated drift.
📡
Ball Detection
REV 2m Distance Sensor
Two sensors positioned at the intake and transfer detect artifact position within the robot, enabling automated game-piece logic.
💡
Indicator
GoBilda RGB Indicator
Color-coded light system shows the number of artifacts loaded in the ramp, giving the driver instant situational awareness.
🧠
Control
REV Control Hub
Android-based hub running Java via FTC SDK. Manages all subsystem communication, autonomous execution, and real-time sensor processing.

Outreach & community

Fear of Bot is rooted in community. This season we ran multiple outreach events across Frederick County, bringing hands-on robotics experiences to families, letting community members drive our robot, and raising awareness of the FIRST program among the next generation of engineers.

Our future plans include hosting an FLL workshop for the ~125 interested contacts we collected, and mentoring a new FLL team to bring more kids into competitive robotics.
